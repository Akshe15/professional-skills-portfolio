Introduction

About This Portfolio Website

How I designed it

I designed this portfolio as a single-page professional website with smooth navigation. The main menu guides users to Home, My Self, Introduction, What I Learn and Contact. Inside the learning section, another lecture menu helps users quickly open each lecture summary.

Languages used

The front end is built with HTML for structure, CSS for colors, layout, floral patterns and responsive design, and JavaScript for lecture switching, mobile navigation, contact form response and scroll animation.

Why this idea

I chose a soft floral theme because it matches my personality and gives the website a friendly look. For the Grow Green project, I used a separate forest-green style to highlight our plant giveaway theme and the environmental message behind it.

What users can get

Users can understand my learning progress, view key points from lectures, see student guide tips, explore class memories and contact me using the complaint-style feedback form.

Lecture 1

👩‍🏫 Ishara Raviharee Weerasinghe

Professional Skills and Employability

This lecture introduced professional skills as the transferable soft skills needed in workplace life. It explained that technical knowledge is important, but communication, teamwork, adaptability, leadership and time management make a person employable and reliable.

Key Points

  • Professional skills support career readiness.
  • Employability skills include communication, problem solving, teamwork and adaptability.
  • Values, beliefs, attitudes and character influence professional behavior.

Student Guide

Build a weekly habit of practicing one soft skill. For example, speak clearly in group work, submit work on time, and reflect on what you can improve after every activity.

Lecture 2

🎤 Guest Lecture

Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to understand, manage and use emotions wisely. The lecture showed how emotions affect relationships, work performance and leadership development.

Key Points

  • Emotions can be seen through voice, facial expression, breathing and body movement.
  • Self-awareness helps us understand our reactions.
  • Managing emotions improves leadership and teamwork.

Student Guide

Before reacting, pause and name the emotion. This small habit helps control stress, avoid conflict and respond professionally.

Lecture 9

🎤 Guest Lecture

CV & Cover Letter Writing

This lecture explained how a CV creates a first impression for employers. A strong CV presents education, skills, achievements and experience in a clear, attractive and job-focused format.

Key Points

  • Use clear headings, bullet points and consistent formatting.
  • Tailor the CV for the job role and company.
  • Avoid spelling mistakes, weak descriptions and unnecessary details.

Student Guide

Keep one master CV and create tailored versions for each job. Match your skills with the job description using honest evidence.

Lecture 8

🎤 Guest Lecture

Interview Skills

The interview skills lecture focused on verbal and non-verbal communication. It taught that interviewers observe knowledge, attitude, body language, listening skills and confidence.

Key Points

  • Prepare answers for strengths, weaknesses and goals.
  • Maintain posture, eye contact and calm tone.
  • Ask meaningful questions about the job and organization.

Student Guide

Practice mock interviews with friends. Record your answer and improve your clarity, confidence and body language.

Lecture 8

🎤 Guest Lecture

Job Hunting and Mock Interview Practice

This lecture connected CV preparation with job search and interview practice. It highlighted the importance of reading job opportunities, preparing CVs, practicing interview questions and using feedback.

Key Points

  • Understand the job market before applying.
  • Use your CV as evidence of your skills.
  • Mock interviews help reduce fear and improve confidence.

Student Guide

Create a small job tracker with company name, role, deadline, CV version and interview preparation notes.

Lecture 5

👩‍🏫 Ms. Oshini Wimalasena

Grooming & Etiquette

This lecture explained that grooming is not about looking expensive or perfect. It is about being clean, neat, appropriate and respectful. Etiquette is how we make others feel through our behavior.

Key Points

  • Grooming includes hygiene, clothing, shoes, posture and neatness.
  • Etiquette includes greeting, listening, phone use and respect.
  • First impressions are shaped by appearance, punctuality, tone and confidence.

Student Guide

Before a professional event, check clothing, hair, shoes, posture and punctuality. Simple neatness can support your talent.

Lecture 6

🎤 Guest Lecture

Dining Etiquette

This lesson taught how table manners help us behave confidently in casual meals, formal dinners, interviews and networking situations. Dining etiquette shows respect for hosts, guests and cultures.

Key Points

  • Use the BMW rule: Bread left, Meal center, Water right.
  • Use utensils from outside to inside.
  • Keep phone away and use polite words with servers.

Student Guide

In formal dining, observe first, follow the host, and keep movements calm. Respectful behavior matters more than perfection.

Lecture 7

🎤 Guest Lecture

Email Etiquette

This topic focused on professional communication through email and phone calls. It reminded us that tone, clarity, structure and politeness are important because they represent our personal and professional image.

Key Points

  • Email needs a clear subject, greeting, message body and closing.
  • Use polite and concise language.
  • Telephone etiquette needs a friendly tone, active listening and clear information.

Student Guide

Before sending an email, read it once as the receiver. Check spelling, tone and whether the action required is clear.

Lecture 10

👩‍🏫 S. Maneesha Aishcharya

Negotiation Skills

Negotiation skills help people reach fair decisions while protecting relationships. The topic encouraged preparation, listening, understanding interests and finding win-win solutions.

Key Points

  • Prepare your goal, limits and alternatives before negotiating.
  • Listen to understand the other side's need.
  • Stay calm and focus on the problem, not the person.

Student Guide

Use the sentence: “I understand your point; can we find a solution that works for both of us?” This keeps negotiation respectful.

Lecture 3

👩‍🏫 Nushkan Nismi

Professional Responsibilities & Code of Ethics

This lecture showed that IT professionals must act responsibly because technology affects privacy, security, fairness and trust. Ethical behavior protects users, organizations and society.

Key Points

  • Respect privacy and protect confidential information.
  • Avoid misuse of systems, data and access rights.
  • Build technology that is fair, honest and safe.

Student Guide

When making software, ask: Is it safe? Is it honest? Does it respect the user? If the answer is unclear, review the ethical risk.

Lecture 4

👨‍🏫 Shenal Rajakarunanayake

Business Ethics in Practice

Business ethics focused on integrity, objectivity and professional conduct. The lecture used workplace examples such as gifts, misleading reports and conflicts of interest to show why ethical decisions matter.

Key Points

  • Integrity means being honest and truthful in professional relationships.
  • Objectivity means avoiding bias and conflict of interest.
  • Professionals must protect the reputation of their field.

Student Guide

If a decision feels hidden, unfair or misleading, stop and check the rules, ask for advice and choose transparency.

Lecture 11

👩‍🏫 Ishara Raviharee Weerasinghe

Portfolio Development

Portfolio writing taught how to collect learning evidence, achievements, projects and reflections. A good portfolio supports personal branding, job applications and academic growth.

Key Points

  • Portfolios show skills through real evidence.
  • Reflection explains what was learned and how it can be improved.
  • Design and organization make the portfolio easy to understand.

Student Guide

Do not only upload work. Add a small reflection for each item explaining your role, challenge, solution and learning outcome.

Group Project

Instead of only doing an ordinary activity, we designed a meaningful plant giveaway with the message “Small Plant, Big Impact”. Project Key Points

  • Project Name: Grow Green.
  • Activity: Free snake plant giveaway at Turret Tower, 4th Floor, Room 403.
  • Purpose: Encourage small eco-friendly actions and promote cleaner indoor surroundings.
  • Team Work: Planning, poster design, decoration, plant arrangement, photography and participant engagement.

This project helped me understand teamwork, creativity, event planning and communication. It also showed that a simple idea can become meaningful when the team gives it purpose, visual identity and clear message.

Final Reflection

My Personal Growth Journey

Looking back at how this module shaped who I am becoming as a professional.

The Professional Skills module helped me understand that career success depends not only on technical knowledge, but also on communication, professionalism, ethics, confidence, emotional intelligence and continuous personal development. As an IT student, I previously focused mainly on technical areas such as programming, web development and software projects. However, through this module, I realised that professional behaviour and soft skills are equally important in the workplace.

The most impactful session for me was Emotional Intelligence because it helped me understand how emotions influence communication, teamwork and decision-making. I learned that a professional should not only be technically capable, but also emotionally aware, respectful and able to manage pressure. Grooming and Etiquette also changed the way I think about first impressions, personal branding and professional appearance.

The sessions on Interview Skills, CV Writing and Email Etiquette were directly useful for my career preparation. I learned how to present myself clearly, write professional emails, improve my CV and prepare for internship interviews. The Code of Ethics and Business Ethics sessions helped me understand the responsibility of IT professionals when handling data, users, systems and organisational information.

Overall, this module improved my confidence, communication, professionalism and career readiness. I will apply these lessons when facing interviews, working in teams, communicating with clients and developing software systems responsibly. This digital portfolio itself represents my learning journey and shows how I have reflected on each lecture and applied the knowledge gained throughout the Professional Skills module.
